Recipe: Stir Fry

This is my take on stir fry, the most important part of this tutorial is the chicken, anything else can be modified or swapped out. Today we will be adding white rice and brocoli to this chicken.

Ingredients

  • Chicken Breast (1lb)
  • Corn Starch (2tbsp)
  • Vegetable Oil (3tbsp)
  • Water (2tbsp)
  • Oyster Sauce (3tbsp)
  • Toasted Sesame Oil(1tsp)
  • Soy Sauce(1tbsp)
  • Sugar (1tsp)
  • Garlic (2 cloves)
  • Chile Pepper Flakes
  • Salt and Pepper To Taste

Optional Add Ons

  • Steamed White/Brown Rice
  • Brocoli
  • Green Beans
  • Chow Mein or Lo Mein
  • Hot Sauce, I recommend Sriracha

Tools Needed

  • Wok or Frying Pan
  • Cutting Board, Knife
  • Small Bowl or Cup
  • Medium Bowl
  • Chopsticks or Fork
  • Measuring Spoons

Good to Know

  • Prep Time:6 minutes
  • Cook Time: 8+ minutes
  • Total Time: 14+ minutes
  • Yields: 4 servings

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in the wok or frying pan over medium-high heat.
  2. If necessary, dice your chicken into bite-sized pieces. Place in a medium bowl and add the corn starch. Toss the chicken making sure all parts are completely covered in corn starch.
  3. Add chicken to pan, cook for around 8 minutes or until the outside looks light brown. Toss occasionally or flip the chicken using chopsticks.
  4. While the chicken is cooking, Add the following to a small bowl or cup: water, oyster sauce, toasted sesame oil, soy sauce, sugar, garlic, chile flakes, salt, & pepper. Mix well.
  5. Add the sauce to the chicken and cook for 5 minutes. The sauce should thicken a bit and look like a glaze. You can also check if it's ready using a cooking thermometer. Chicken should reach an internal temp of 165℉ or 74℃.
  6. At this point you can add your base (like rice or noodles) and veggies directly to the pan and toss for another minute or assemble it separately in a bowl.

Preparation

Add base, veggies, and chicken to a bowl. Add any toppings you would like, I'm a fan of hot sauce.

Final Result

This is a quick and easy recipe that you can dress up or down. You can also replace the glaze with pre-made sauce. I recommend the orange chicken sauce from panda express, its sold in some local grocery stores and online.
