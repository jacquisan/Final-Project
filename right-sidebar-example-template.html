Mexican Chicken Tinga

This Crowd-Favorite Is A Classic Mexican Dish And For Good Reason

Simple but effective- this dish will not disappoint.

Tinga is a classic Mexican dish, it can be made with chicken or pork. It is a simple dish and you probably have most of the ingredients at home. The level of spice can also be adjusted based on personal preference.

Ingredients

  • Chicken Breast (1lb)
  • Roma Tomatoes (3)
  • White Onion (1.5)
  • Garlic (2-3 cloves)
  • 1 can of Chipotle Peppers in Adobo sauce
  • Cooking Oil, Corn or Vegetable (2tbsp)
  • Chicken Bouillon (1 cube) or Chicken Broth (5 cups)
  • Bay Leaf, fresh or dry (1)
  • Salt and Pepper

Optional, but highly recommended:

  • Flat Tostadas
  • White Crumbling Cheese, I recommend Mexican Queso Fresco
  • Sour Cream
  • Avocado
  • Hot Sauce, I recommend an Avocado Salsa Verde

Tools Needed

  • Blender or NutriBullet
  • Medium Pot
  • Medium to Large Saute Pan
  • Cutting Board & Knives
  • Two Forks

Good to Know

  • Prep Time:10 minutes
  • Cook Time: 80-90 minutes
  • Total Time: 90-100 minutes
  • Yields: 4 servings

Steps

  1. In a medium pot, bring 5 cups of water (or broth) to a boil. Add the chicken, a quarter of an onion, the bay leaf, 1 garlic clove, and salt & pepper to taste. If you did not use chicken broth, add 1 cube of chicken bouillon. Lower the temperature to a rolling boil. Cook for 40-50 minutes. If you would like to speed up the process, feel free to split the chicken in half or quarters beforehand.
  2. Remove the chicken from the heat. You can wait for the chicken to cool while you continue with the other steps or if you're impatient you can shred the chicken now using two forks. It's ok if the chicken is not fully cooked, it will finish cooking in the sauce later on.
    • Optional: If your spice tolerance is low you must scrape out the seeds of the chipotle peppers. On a cutting board carefully slice the peppers down the middle and scoop out the seeds using a spoon. If you would like some spice, set aside a small amount of seeds, you can add those in the next step. Remember, do not touch your eyes.
  3. In a blender add: 3 tomatoes sliced in quarters, the other quarter of the onion, 1-2 garlic cloves, the chipotle peppers with the adobo sauce, and half a cup of the chicken broth (remember to take out the bay leaf). Blend until smooth.
  4. Cut one onion into medium to thin slices, lengthwise. Check out this great tutorial from Cooking Guide called, "Food Cutting and Slicing: How to Thinly Slice an Onion".
  5. Heat the oil in the large saute pan over medium heat. When the oil is shimmering, add the onion slices. When they become transluscent in color, add the tomato-chipotle sauce from the blender. Heat the sauce for 5 minutes then add the shredded chicken. Simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the chicken is soft and tender.
    • Optional: I like to remove the chicken from the heat and wait for the sauce to absorb into the chicken. This is essential if you are serving the tinga on tostadas or you may have to strain the chicken from the liquid.

Preparation

On a tostada, add the chicken and the optional toppings. Sour cream first, cheese, avocado, and finally the salsa.

Final Notes

See? It wasn't that difficult. Besides the simplicity of this dish, the best part is that you can easily reheat the leftovers, and it's always a crowd pleaser.
